Key Characteristics of Services

The Characteristics of Services

  1. Intangibilty: The Intangibilty makes services unable to be touched or sensed
  2. Inseparability: The Inseparability of services reflects the interconnection among the service provider, the customer involved in receiving the service, and other customer sharing the service experience
  3. Heterogeneity: The heterogeneity of services reflects the variation in consistency from on services transaction to the next
  4. Perishability: A distinguishing feature of services is that they cannot be saved, their unused capacity cannot be reserved, and they cannot be inventoried

Marketing problem caused by intangibility

  1. Lack of ability to be stored: Due to thier intangibility, services can not be inventoried.
  2. Lack of proction by patents: Because of the property intangibility, services are not patentable. Human labor and effort are not protected
  3. Services cannot be readily displayed or communicated: The promotion of services is a challenge for the marketers.
    For example: Insurance policy
  4. Difficulty in pricing: The challenge involved in pricing of services is that there is no cost of goods sold. The primary cost of producing a service is labor.

Possible solutions to Intangibility problems

  1. The use of tangible clues: Due to thier intangibility, services can not be inventoried.
  2. The use of personal sources information: Because of the property intangibility, services are not patentable. Human labor and effort are not protected
  3. Creation of strong organizational image: The promotion of services is a challenge for the marketers.
    For example: Insurance policy
